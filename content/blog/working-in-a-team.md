One of the most transformative parts of my programming journey was learning how to work with a team, which I did throughout my senior year of high school. This was a major step from just working on solo projects, and though it came with many challenges, it also brought some valuable rewards. 

Among the most important takeaways I had from working in a team was the importance of feedback. When working on solo projects, I had more control over what the final product would look like, but getting feedback was also much more difficult compared to how it is when working for a team since I didn't have anybody to bounce off of. When I worked in a team, on the other hand, I would often get feedback about how to improve my coding practices, which made it easy to recognize where I went wrong on a technical level and allowed me to spot and fix mistakes I would have otherwise missed. 

This also makes it easier to avoid technical debt because mistakes are easier to spot and fix when there are teammates around to give feedback on your work. When I worked on my solo projects, it was easier for a mistake I made on one project to carry over to the next, but this was easier to avert when working in a team.

Communication is also one of the most important things I took away from this experience. Better communication allows teamwork to go much more smoothly, and teammates need to be able to bounce ideas off of one another and notify each other if any problems arise ahead of time in order for their teams to function well. In other words, good communication translates to good collaboration, which is essential for teamwork. It was also one of the challenges I faced working in a team; communication didn't take center stage when working solo, but it was highly relevant when working in a group. Hence, I needed to be responsible for notifying my teammates of any problems, any issues I had, places where I needed help, etc. Team projects tend to be on larger scales than solo projects, and realizing that each teammate played a specific role (for example, backend) in the group project I was a part of, I realized that depending on what I needed help with, I could ask different group members for solutions to any problems I had, and each teammate knew whom to ask for any given issue. Knowing this helped me overcome that challenge.

For those reasons, communication is one of the challenges a team needs to meet in order to function well and work efficiently. A team that communicates poorly is going to make much less progress compared to working solo, but one that communicates well can easily cover for each member's strengths and weaknesses, resulting in more progress being made.

Communication also happens to be one of the skills employers value the most. They agree that soft skills matter as much as hard (technical) skills do, and having good soft skills makes it much easier to work with other employees. When it comes to professions, the ability to communicate and explain your ideas to someone else is among the best knowledge one can have. Working for a company almost always involves working in a team; good interpersonal skills are an asset, and bad interpersonal skills are a liability. The ability to communicate problems or issues before they arise is also highly important. Learning these skills made me more confident in my abilities as a programmer and as a teammate, which I can easily attribute to teamwork; these would have been much harder to learn had I just done solo projects.

That is what these experiences meant to me, and that is how these takeaways helped me grow as a person and as a programmer.
